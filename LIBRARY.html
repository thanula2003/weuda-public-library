About Weuda Library

Established in 1972, Weuda Public Library has been serving the community for over five decades. Our mission is to provide free and open access to information, knowledge, and cultural resources for all members of our community.

Our beautiful facility spans over 20,000 square feet and houses more than 100,000 books, periodicals, and multimedia resources. We take pride in being more than just a repository of books—we are a vibrant community hub.

Our dedicated team of librarians and staff are committed to creating a welcoming environment where curiosity is nurtured, learning is celebrated, and connections are forged.

Our Services

Weuda Public Library offers a wide range of services to meet the diverse needs of our community.

Reading Rooms

Quiet, comfortable spaces for study and reading, with natural lighting and ergonomic furniture.

Computer Access

Free computer and internet access for all members, with printing and scanning facilities.

Community Programs

Regular events including author talks, book clubs, children's storytime, and educational workshops.

Meeting Spaces

Reservable rooms for community groups, study sessions, and organizational meetings.

Research Assistance

Expert help from our librarians for academic, professional, or personal research projects.

Children's Area

A dedicated space for young readers with age-appropriate books and educational toys.

Take a visual journey through our beautiful facilities and vibrant community events.
